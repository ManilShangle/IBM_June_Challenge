# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Football fans, commentators, and broadcasters watching a live VAR review unfold in real time. They are engaged, impatient, and want to know the outcome before the referee announces it. They have varying levels of knowledge about the Laws of the Game.

## Product Purpose

Predict the most likely VAR ruling before it is officially revealed, grounded in the IFAB Laws of the Game. The app takes real match intake (team names, a situation description, and optional footage) and returns a predicted ruling with a confidence score, cited law, and transparent rationale. Success is a fan who sees the prediction and feels informed rather than guessing.

## Brand Personality

Bold, instant, decisive. Referee authority, not betting energy. The tool should feel like a trusted analyst calling it straight, not a pundit hedging.

## Anti-references

- Clinical white hospital UI: lifeless, zero-contrast pure-white shells (think generic SaaS forms)
- Sports betting or gambling app aesthetics: odds boards, neon greens, dark high-contrast countdown timers
- IBM blue corporate deck: heavy branding that feels like a sales presentation rather than a tool

## Design Principles

1. The ruling dominates. Every other element is supporting evidence, not competing decoration.
2. Confidence is a real signal. Calibrated, visible, honest — not a percentage badge for show.
3. Transparency earns trust. The cited law text is always one click away; the prediction is never a black box.
4. The tool disappears into the task. Inputs are minimal and unobtrusive; the verdict is maximal and immediate.
5. No ambiguity in states. Positive, negative, and uncertain calls have visually distinct, unambiguous treatment.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. IBM Plex Sans covers readability. Semantic color use only (colors carry meaning, not decoration). No motion gating of visible content.
